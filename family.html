Introduction: Pioneers of Cobb County

The Lassiter family holds a significant and longstanding position in the historical landscape of Marietta, Georgia, and the broader community of Cobb County. The family's enduring legacy is most notably linked to civic contributions, land development, and a profound dedication to education, particularly through the life of Leamond Narvel Lassiter.

Central to this legacy is Leamond Narvel Lassiter (1879–1966), whose extensive service on the Cobb County Board of Education and advocacy for school consolidation cemented the family's place in the region's history. The Lassiters lived through major social and economic shifts, and their descendants have continued to contribute to the community, including attending the high school named in their honor.

Genealogical Overview: The Lassiter Family Branches

Two Prominent Branches of the Lassiter Legacy

The Lassiter family in Marietta is represented by two historically significant branches, both making substantial contributions to Cobb County's African American community and history.

I. The East Cobb County Branch (L.N. Lassiter Family)

This branch is notably recognized for its substantial contributions to Cobb County's educational and business history, honored by the naming of Lassiter High School.

Founding Generation

Leamond Narvel Lassiter (1879–1966)
Pioneer East Cobb family member whose significant contributions to education resulted in the naming of Lassiter High School in his honor.

Eva Mae (Hagood) Lassiter (1887–1984)
Wife of L.N. Lassiter, Sr. The couple raised nine children who carried on the family legacy.

Notable Descendants

Harry Lamar Lassiter (1926-2006)
Son - Local business leader, World War II Navy veteran, and co-ran the Lassiter Family businesses.

Johnnie LeCroy Lassiter
Daughter-in-law (Wife of Harry) - Matriarch of the Lassiter Family businesses, Co-Manager of Lassiter Property Investment, and a lifelong leader in the Marietta Campmeeting community.

L.N. Lassiter, Jr. (1922-2004)
Son - Notable community member in Marietta and Americus, married to Charlotte Casey Lassiter.

Charlotte Casey Lassiter
Daughter-in-law - Worked at the Bell Aircraft bomber plant during WWII and was active in Mt. Zion churches and the Marietta Camp Meeting.

II. The Lemon Street District Branch (Isaac and Mary Lassiter)

This branch established roots in the African American Lemon Street District of Marietta after the Civil War, beginning with their property purchase on Lemon Street in 1898.

Founding Generation

Isaac and Mary Lassiter
Established the family's long-term legacy through property ownership and community involvement in the historic Lemon Street District.

Notable Descendant

Patrice Shelton Lassiter
A noted historian and contributor to the historical record of Black schools in Cobb County, including documenting the history of the first African American teacher at Lemon Street Elementary.

The Lassiter family's legacy continues through their descendants and the lasting impact of their contributions to education and community development in Cobb County.

Family Properties and Residences

East Cobb County

The L.N. Lassiter family maintained strong ties to East Cobb County, with properties that supported their business ventures and community involvement. Their legacy is most visibly preserved through Lassiter High School, established in 1981 in their honor.

Lemon Street District

The Isaac and Mary Lassiter branch established their legacy through property ownership in the historic African American Lemon Street District, beginning with their purchase in 1898. This area became a significant center of African American life in Marietta.

Both branches of the Lassiter family demonstrate the importance of property ownership and community building in the African American experience in Cobb County, with legacies that continue to influence the area today.

Historical Context

The Lassiter family's story is one of adaptation and persistence within Cobb County. The East Cobb branch's educational advocacy and the Lemon Street branch's community leadership both played crucial roles in shaping Marietta's development, particularly within the African American community during the era of segregation and subsequent desegregation.

Family Members and Relationships

Leamond N. Lassiter (1879-1966)

Leamond N. Lassiter was the central figure who earned the family's lasting recognition through his public service. He married Eva Mae Hagood (1887-1984) on February 15, 1903. They had nine children who continued the family's legacy of community service and leadership.

Eva Mae Hagood Lassiter (1887-1984)

Eva Mae Hagood was the wife of Leamond N. Lassiter and the matriarch of the family line honored by the school naming. She outlived Leamond, passing away in 1984, and was instrumental in maintaining family connections and traditions.

Isaac and Mary Lassiter

Founders of the Lemon Street District branch, Isaac and Mary Lassiter established their family's presence in Marietta through strategic property acquisition and community involvement, setting the stage for future generations' contributions.

Enduring Legacy

The Lassiter family's impact on Cobb County continues to be felt today through Lassiter High School, the historical record preserved by family historians, and the ongoing contributions of their descendants to the Marietta community. Their story exemplifies the power of education, property ownership, and community engagement in creating lasting change.

Foundational Records and Family Background

Leamond Narvel Lassiter Sr. (April 18, 1879 – January 7, 1966) and Eva Mae Hagood Lassiter (February 23, 1887 – August 5, 1984) were married on February 15, 1903, in Cobb County, Georgia.
